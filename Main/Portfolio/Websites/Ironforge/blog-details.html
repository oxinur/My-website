The Complete Guide to Pre- and Post-Workout Nutrition

  • by Priya Sharma
  • Apr 3, 2025
  • 22 Comments

What you eat around your training sessions can make or break your progress. Whether your goal is to build muscle, lose body fat, or improve athletic performance, timing and composition of your nutrition is a critical variable that too many people overlook. This guide breaks down the evidence and gives you clear, practical guidelines to follow.

Research consistently shows that consuming a moderate portion of carbohydrates and protein 60–90 minutes before training improves performance output and reduces muscle breakdown. Post-workout, a protein-rich meal or shake within two hours of finishing is widely regarded as optimal for muscle protein synthesis — though total daily intake remains the most important factor.

Pre-Workout: What to Eat and When

Aim for a balanced meal 60–90 minutes before training, providing roughly 25–40g of protein and 40–60g of carbohydrates. Good options include chicken and rice, Greek yogurt with oats, or a banana with a protein shake. Avoid high-fat or high-fibre meals close to training as these slow digestion and can cause discomfort during exercise.

Within two hours of completing your session, prioritise a protein source alongside fast-digesting carbohydrates to replenish glycogen stores and trigger muscle repair. A shake with 25–30g of whey protein and a piece of fruit is a convenient option. Whole food alternatives like eggs on toast or a salmon and sweet potato bowl are excellent if your schedule permits.

Fuelling your training is not complicated — but it does require consistency. Small, smart choices made every day add up to significant performance gains over months and years.
PRIYA SHARMA — IronForge Nutrition Coach

For most recreational gym-goers, the biggest nutritional wins come not from obsessing over the 30-minute post-workout window, but from ensuring adequate total daily protein intake (1.6–2.2g per kg of bodyweight) and sufficient overall calories to support your training demands.

If you're unsure where to start, book a session with one of our in-house nutrition coaches at IronForge. We offer complimentary consultations for all new members and can put together a simple, sustainable nutrition plan that fits around your lifestyle — no extreme diets, no unnecessary supplements, just evidence-based guidance that works.
